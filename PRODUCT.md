# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Genesee County Land Bank Authority compliance staff use this tool during desk research. They work through exported property lists, compare property imagery, record manual findings, and decide which homes need physical inspection.

## Product Purpose

Compliance Tracker is a local CSV-in, CSV-out triage tool for reviewing non-respondent properties. It helps staff search, sort, inspect imagery, upload before and after evidence, and export review findings without connecting to FileMaker or any GCLBA system.

Success means staff can trust the queue, understand which records are reviewed, see photo evidence at useful sizes, and make program-aware compliance decisions without overstated automation.

## Brand Personality

Civic, plainspoken, exact. The interface should feel like a dependable government desk tool: calm enough for repeated use, dense enough for operational work, and careful about uncertainty.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing language, decorative dashboards, SaaS-style hero metrics, AI-branded claims, buyer-facing flows, and any interface that implies detection is a final assessment. Avoid tiny evidence thumbnails, cluttered cards, hidden upload destinations, and status math that treats unknown records as non-compliant.

## Design Principles

- Evidence first: imagery, address identity, and review state should be easier to scan than decorative summary text.
- Honest progress: dashboard metrics must distinguish reviewed, pending, photo-ready, compliant, and unresolved work.
- Program-aware judgment: compliance meaning changes by program, so finding displays must keep program context nearby.
- CSV boundary stays visible: imports and exports remain explicit staff actions, with no direct FileMaker or GCLBA integration.
- Human final call: automated labels are only likely triage signals, never confirmed outcomes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA behavior for labels, focus-visible states, keyboard search, readable responsive layouts, and color contrast. Motion should be minimal and state-driven. Information must not depend on color alone, especially compliance outcome and photo completeness.
